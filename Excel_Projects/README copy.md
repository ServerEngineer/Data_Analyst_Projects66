# Excel_projects
These are three Excel projects, professionally classified as Paginated Reports designed in a stakeholder-focused reporting style.
The projects make extensive use of advanced Excel functionalities, including combining multiple tables using Power Pivot for robust data modeling.
A Star Schema was implemented with one fact table and multiple dimension tables, ensuring accurate relationships and scalability.
Advanced DAX formulas were extensively used within the Power Pivot environment, along with the Pivot Data Model to integrate multiple tables and enable high-performance analytics.
These three projects are the direct outcome of applying advanced Excel, data modeling, and DAX capabilities effectively.

P_1 🎯 Business Objectives

Track Net Sales growth across years and customers
Compare actual performance vs targets at market level
Identify top and bottom performing products
Analyze division-wise and country-wise contribution
Highlight new product impact in 2021

P_2 🎯 Business Objectives

Monitor Profit & Loss (P&L) performance across fiscal years without altering source pivot tables
Track Net Sales, COGS, Gross Margin, and GM% at monthly, quarterly, and yearly levels
Analyze year-over-year sales growth (2019–2021) to understand recovery and expansion trends
Identify margin pressure and cost efficiency trends through GM% movement across years
Compare market-wise profitability to spot high-revenue vs high-margin regions
Evaluate sub-zone and quarter-wise margin consistency to assess regional operational stability
Detect seasonality patterns in sales and margins across months and quarters
Support management decisions on pricing, cost control, and regional focus using margin insights
Enable performance benchmarking across markets, sub-zones, and fiscal periods

P_3 🎯 Business Objectives
Evaluate multiple business functions using a structured priority matrix
Measure initiatives across Feasibility, Impact, and Size dimensions
Identify high-impact, high-feasibility areas for immediate execution
Classify initiatives into Major, Quick Wins, Maybe Later, and Nice-to-Have categories
Optimize resource allocation by focusing on high-priority functions
Reduce decision bias by using quantitative scoring
